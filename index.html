Tour De Maths Archive

Developing young mathematicians through collaboration, problem-solving and competition.

Tour De Maths is a Johannesburg-based mathematics competition that has been running since 2005, with the aim of developing mathematical thinking in Grade 8, 9 and 10 learners.

Schools send 16 learners to each event. Pairs of learners from one school are partnered with a pair from another school, forming teams of four who work together on an hour-long mathematics paper. The questions draw on curriculum knowledge, creative problem-solving and collaborative thinking.

At the end of each event, a prize-giving is held on the night. Across the seven legs of the year, results are recorded to recognise the top schools and leading participants.

The Tour De Maths programme is currently administered and managed by St Stithians Boys' College on behalf of participating Johannesburg schools.

For enquiries regarding the competition, hosting, participation or archive resources, please contact Eugene Stolk at estolk@stithian.com.

How To Use This Archive

This archive is intended to support learners, teachers and schools in developing mathematical problem-solving beyond the standard classroom curriculum.

Learners are encouraged to attempt the papers before consulting the memoranda. The greatest value comes from working through unfamiliar problems, discussing possible approaches, and returning to challenging questions after reflection.

Teachers may use these papers for enrichment lessons, extension groups, team preparation, problem-solving clubs or informal mathematical challenges.
